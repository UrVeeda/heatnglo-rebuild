## Direct answer

A gas insert conversion retrofits a wood-burning fireplace opening with a direct-vent gas appliance without demolishing the existing chimney or masonry. The process involves:

1. **Consultation and measurement** -- your authorized dealer measures the existing opening and confirms which insert model fits
2. **Liner installation** -- a stainless steel liner runs from the insert through the existing chimney chase to the termination cap at the top
3. **Gas line connection** -- a licensed contractor extends the gas line to the firebox location if it isn't already there
4. **Insert installation** -- the insert slides into the opening, connects to the liner and gas supply, and the surround trim covers the seam
5. **Final inspection** -- the installation is inspected to code before first use

The result: a sealed, direct-vent gas appliance that operates on demand, produces no ash, and requires no chimney management. The existing masonry stays in place.

## What stays and what changes

| Element | What happens during conversion |
|---|---|
| Existing masonry firebox | Stays -- insert slides into it |
| Existing chimney structure | Stays -- new liner runs through it |
| Existing chimney flue | Retired for combustion; liner takes over |
| Existing damper | Typically locked open or removed during install |
| Gas line | New or extended to firebox location |
| Electrical connection | New rough-in for IntelliFire ignition system |
| Hearth and surround | Retained or updated -- insert trim covers the seam |

## The real reason people convert

The honest version: most wood-burning fireplaces in existing homes stopped getting used because the maintenance cost exceeded the enjoyment. A sweep. A screen. The ash. The kindling. The fifteen minutes of setup for twenty minutes of fire.

A gas insert addresses all of it. Push a button. There's a fire. Push it again. It's off. No ash management. No chimney sweeping schedule (though the liner still benefits from periodic inspection). The fireplace becomes something the household actually uses because using it costs almost no effort.

Families with young children or pets find the sealed glass panel a meaningful safety improvement over an open wood-burning firebox with live embers.

## The conversion process step by step

### Step 1: Consultation and opening measurement

Your authorized Heat & Glo dealer visits the home, measures the existing firebox opening, and identifies which insert model fits correctly. Insert sizing is precise -- the unit must fit the opening with adequate clearance for the surround trim to cover the gap neatly. This is not a "close enough" measurement.

The dealer also assesses the chimney condition. The existing flue doesn't need to be in perfect working order -- it just needs to be structurally sound enough to serve as the chase for the new liner. A sweep or structural repair may be recommended before the liner install.

![Heat & Glo HTI gas insert with stainless liner and black metal finish in a traditional opening](https://hearthnhome.getbynder.com/transform/1e9d4ffe-5f0d-4b27-a484-09432f4af30a/HTI-PIER-DV36IN_blackmetal-and-logs?io=transform:fill,width:1200,height:680)

### Step 2: Liner installation

A flexible stainless steel liner drops from the top of the chimney down to the firebox level. The liner connects to the insert's exhaust port and the co-axial vent assembly. For direct-vent inserts, the liner carries both the exhaust out and the combustion air supply in -- the co-axial design handles both functions in a single pipe assembly.

This is the part that distinguishes a gas insert from a gas log set. A log set sits in the existing firebox and uses the existing flue. An insert replaces the combustion zone entirely with a sealed appliance and a dedicated liner.

### Step 3: Gas line connection

If a gas line doesn't already run to the firebox location, a licensed contractor extends the line from the nearest supply point. This is standard rough-in work, and your authorized dealer can coordinate with a qualified gas contractor who has experience with fireplace installations.

Propane installations follow the same process with the appropriate regulator and connection hardware.

### Step 4: Insert installation and trim

The insert slides into the firebox opening. The vent connections are made. Electrical rough-in for the IntelliFire ignition system is confirmed. The decorative surround trim -- typically a steel or cast-iron frame -- covers the gap between the insert's face and the existing masonry opening. The finish is clean. The existing masonry character is preserved where it extends beyond the trim.

## What to expect after conversion

The fireplace gets used. That's the consistent feedback from households that complete the conversion. A fireplace that required fifteen minutes of setup and an hour of cleanup sits unused. A fireplace that lights on demand gets used on Tuesday evenings, not just holiday weekends.

The glass panel stays cleaner than a wood-burning firebox. The flame presentation on a gas insert with log media or glass media is consistent and controlled -- no variable flame behavior based on wood moisture content or draft conditions.

Annual maintenance is simpler: the liner benefits from periodic inspection, and the unit should be serviced on a schedule your dealer recommends. But the frequency and complexity of maintenance are meaningfully lower than a wood-burning fireplace with active chimney use.

## How to choose between an insert and a full replacement

If the existing masonry opening is structurally sound and correctly proportioned, an insert is usually the right call. It preserves what's there and adds modern gas appliance performance.

If the existing opening is damaged, incorrectly sized for the room, or if the design intent calls for a contemporary linear firebox that the existing masonry proportion can't accommodate, a full replacement -- demolition of the existing firebox and surround, new framing, and a new Heat & Glo gas fireplace -- may be the better investment.

Your authorized dealer can assess both paths during the consultation and make a recommendation based on the specific opening.

## Frequently asked questions

### How long does a gas insert conversion take from consultation to first fire?

The timeline depends on scheduling, gas line work if needed, and permit review in your jurisdiction. A straightforward conversion where a gas line already exists and the opening is the right size can complete in a matter of weeks. A project that requires a gas line extension or liner special-order may take longer. Your dealer can give you a project-specific timeline estimate during the consultation.

### Will a gas insert heat my home effectively?

A gas insert delivers direct radiant heat through the glass panel. For the immediate room, the warmth is real and usable. Gas inserts are not designed as whole-home heating systems -- they're zone-heating appliances. If your goal is to meaningfully offset your central heating system in the room where the fireplace is located, a properly sized gas insert accomplishes that.

### Does my existing chimney need to be in good condition?

The chimney structure needs to be sound enough to serve as a chase for the liner. The flue liner condition is less critical because the new stainless liner takes over the combustion function. Your dealer's consultation includes a chimney assessment and can recommend any required structural repairs before the insert installation.

### Can I keep the existing surround and mantel?

Usually yes. The insert's decorative trim covers the gap between the insert face and the existing masonry. If the existing surround extends beyond that trim zone, it stays in place. If you want to update the surround as part of the conversion, your dealer can recommend Heat & Glo accessory options that pair with the insert.

### Is the $650 rebate available everywhere?

The rebate is available through participating Heat & Glo authorized dealers. Availability, participating dealers, and qualifying products are confirmed at the point of consultation. Ask your dealer at the time of your free in-home visit.

### Do I need a permit for a gas insert conversion?

Yes, in most jurisdictions. The gas line extension and new gas appliance installation require permits pulled by the licensed contractors doing the work. Your authorized Heat & Glo dealer manages this as part of a standard installation. Do not attempt to bypass the permit process -- it affects insurance coverage and resale disclosure requirements.
