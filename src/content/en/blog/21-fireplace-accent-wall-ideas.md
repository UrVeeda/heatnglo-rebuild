The fireplace is the room's anchor. The accent wall is the room's commitment. When the two are designed together, the fireplace stops being a feature and starts being the reason the room exists.

Twenty-one ideas, organized by material family.

## Stone (1 to 7)
1. Floor-to-ceiling continuous slab in honed limestone
2. Stacked dry-laid fieldstone with Mezzo linear opening
3. Cast stone surround with traditional mantel cap
4. Vertical-grain travertine with hidden lighting at the reveal
5. Charred basalt panels around a Cosmo
6. Rough-face quartzite from hearth to ceiling
7. Reclaimed barn stone in a contemporary frame

## Tile (8 to 14)
8. Large-format porcelain in a single slab look
9. Vertical fluted tile in matte navy
10. Glazed terracotta in a warm white
11. Hand-glazed zellige in graphite
12. Concrete-effect porcelain with brass reveals
13. Thin-set marble book-matched at the firebox center
14. Fluted plaster panels (technically not tile, included for completeness)

## Wood + Mantel (15 to 21)
15. Floating walnut mantel shelf with stone base
16. Reclaimed oak full-height paneling with TV-above-the-mantel
17. White oak slat wall with a Mezzo See-Through
18. Painted shiplap with a traditional 6K/8K Series Traditional
19. Flat-panel painted millwork with hidden bookshelves
20. Limewashed brick with a chunky walnut mantel
21. Continuous wood paneling, fireplace-as-cutout

Specify with your designer. Confirm clearance-to-combustibles requirements with the installation manual for your specific Heat & Glo unit before any mantel material is installed.
