## Direct answer

A gas log set and a gas insert are not interchangeable options for the same situation. Each one solves a different problem:

1. **Gas log set** -- the right choice when the existing firebox is structurally sound, you want to preserve the masonry character, and your primary goal is the look of a real flame without wood management
2. **Gas insert** -- the right choice when you want maximum usable heat, sealed combustion, and a self-contained modern appliance that doesn't rely on the existing flue
3. **The firebox condition decides** -- if the existing masonry is sound, a log set is viable; if the firebox is failing or if sealed combustion is the priority, an insert is the right call

Your authorized dealer assesses the existing opening during a free consultation and tells you which path makes sense for your specific situation.

## When a gas log set is the right answer

A gas log set is the answer when the existing wood-burning firebox is still in good structural condition and the household's primary goal is eliminating wood management while preserving the masonry character of the original opening.

### The existing firebox has real masonry character

Brick, fieldstone, and cast masonry fireboxes often have aesthetic value that the household wants to keep. A log set preserves all of it. The masonry interior stays visible. The proportions of the original opening are unchanged. The log set sits inside the firebox as the new combustion element while the character of what surrounds it remains.

### The goal is the flame look, not the heat output

Gas log sets produce a real flame from a real gas burner. They deliver warmth. But their primary value to most buyers is the aesthetic -- the dancing flame, the glow, the atmosphere -- without the fifteen minutes of wood setup and the ash cleanup.

If the household's primary question is "can we have a fire tonight without dealing with wood?" a log set answers it.

### The household doesn't need sealed combustion

Gas log sets use the existing flue as their exhaust path. They are not sealed systems. Combustion air is drawn from the room; exhaust goes up through the existing chimney. This is a well-understood, code-compliant approach when the flue is in good condition.

For households in jurisdictions with strong indoor air quality standards, or in very tightly sealed modern homes with low air-exchange rates, the open-combustion design of a log set is a consideration to discuss with your dealer.

## When a gas insert is the right answer

A gas insert is the right answer when the household wants a self-contained sealed appliance that delivers more usable heat, operates independently of the existing flue's combustion function, and provides the modern gas fireplace experience rather than a retrofit of what was already there.

### Maximum usable heat output is the priority

A sealed gas insert delivers direct radiant heat through the front glass panel more efficiently than a log set in an open firebox. The sealed combustion chamber concentrates the heat output toward the room rather than losing a portion up the flue. For households converting from wood-burning specifically because they want the fireplace to contribute meaningfully to room heating, an insert is the right path.

### The existing flue is in marginal condition

If the chimney assessment reveals a flue in marginal condition -- deteriorating liner, damaged masonry -- a log set that relies on that flue as the exhaust path is problematic. A gas insert installs a new stainless liner through the existing chimney chase, which doesn't require the original flue to be in pristine condition. The liner takes over the combustion function; the old flue is retired.

### The household wants sealed combustion and cool-glass safety

Sealed glass is one of the defining features of a gas insert. The front glass panel remains significantly cooler than the radiant glass on an open-combustion log set. For households with young children or pets, this distinction matters. The sealed glass panel prevents direct contact with the flame zone while still delivering the visual of a real fire.

### The insert lineup for existing openings

- **Escape** -- wide size range, traditional to transitional aesthetic, one of the most-specified insert families
- **Provident** -- accessible price point, clean presentation, available in multiple opening sizes
- **Supreme and SupremeX** -- premium inserts with larger BTU output, expanded media options, and IntelliFire app integration

## What your existing firebox condition tells you

The condition of the existing masonry is the diagnostic that your dealer uses to point you toward one path or the other.

### Sound masonry: both options are on the table

If the firebox refractory is intact, the damper operates correctly, and the flue liner is in serviceable condition, both a log set and an insert are viable. The decision becomes about goals: aesthetic flame vs. maximum heat output, open combustion vs. sealed.

### Deteriorating or cracked refractory

Cracked refractory panels in the firebox interior should be addressed before any combustion appliance operates in the opening. A gas insert, with its self-contained sealed combustion chamber, is less sensitive to refractory condition than an open-combustion log set. But the assessment still needs to happen before the installation.

### Damaged or deteriorating flue liner

A deteriorating flue liner is a case for the insert, not the log set. The insert's stainless steel liner bypasses the old flue entirely. The log set depends on it.

### Opening size outside the standard range

If the existing opening is unusually wide, unusually narrow, or unusually shallow, some products won't fit correctly. This is the measurement that the dealer's consultation resolves. Don't order product before the measurement is confirmed.

## How to choose: three questions

1. **Do you want to preserve the masonry aesthetic of the existing firebox?** If yes, lean toward a log set. If the existing masonry isn't worth preserving -- or if it's failing -- lean toward an insert.
2. **Is maximum usable heat output a primary goal?** If yes, the insert delivers more of it. If the goal is primarily the flame aesthetic, the log set is sufficient.
3. **What condition is the existing flue in?** Sound flue opens both options. Marginal or failing flue points to the insert.

## Frequently asked questions

### Can a gas log set look as realistic as a real wood fire?

The best gas log sets -- Heritage Oak, Grand Oak, Grand Birch -- have log detail, bark texture, and ember bed presentations that read realistically from a normal viewing distance. They are not identical to a wood fire. The flame pattern is controlled and consistent rather than variable. For most households, the visual is satisfying and the trade-off against wood management is worth it.

### Do gas log sets require a chimney?

Log sets that vent through the existing flue require a functioning chimney. Vent-free log sets -- available from Heat & Glo in jurisdictions where vent-free is permitted -- don't require venting at all. Confirm with your dealer whether vent-free is appropriate for your jurisdiction and your specific installation.

### Does a gas insert look like a gas insert, or does it look like a fireplace?

A well-specified gas insert with the right media -- log set, glass media, or ember bed -- in the right sized opening looks like a fireplace. The decorative surround trim that covers the gap between the insert face and the existing masonry is designed to blend the two elements. High-end insert installations are often indistinguishable from built-from-scratch gas fireplaces.

### Can I upgrade a gas log set to an insert later?

Yes. If you install a log set now and want to convert to an insert later, the process is the same as converting from wood-burning: the existing opening is measured, an insert is selected and sized, a liner is installed, and the log set is removed. The masonry that the log set was sitting in remains.

### Is there a rebate available for gas inserts?

Heat & Glo's Gas Insert Rebate program offers qualifying homeowners up to $650 off a qualifying gas insert upgrade from a wood-burning original. Confirm current rebate availability, participating dealers, and qualifying models with your authorized dealer during the consultation. The rebate is applied at the point of sale -- no mail-in process.

### What's the maintenance difference between a log set and an insert?

Log sets in vented configurations share the chimney maintenance requirement with the original wood-burning fireplace -- annual inspection is recommended. Inserts require periodic liner inspection and appliance service, but the frequency and scope of maintenance are generally lower than an active wood-burning firebox with chimney use. Your dealer can confirm the recommended service schedule for your specific product.
