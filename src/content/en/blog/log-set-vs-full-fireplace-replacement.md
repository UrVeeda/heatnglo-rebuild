You have a wood-burning fireplace and you've decided to convert. The next decision is whether you want a gas log set or a full insert replacement. They look similar in the catalog. They are not similar in what they do.

## A gas log set retrofits the existing opening

A log set sits inside your existing wood-burning firebox. The masonry, the firebox refractory, the existing damper system stay where they are. A burner pan installs at the bottom, the log arrangement sits on top, gas line runs to the burner. The result: real flame, no chimney work, no demolition. The original masonry character is preserved.

The Duzy 2/3/5, Heritage Oak, Grand Oak, Grand Birch, Contemporary, and Kindled Stack are all log set products designed for this scenario.

## A gas insert replaces the firebox itself

An insert is a complete sealed appliance that slides into the existing opening. The insert IS the new firebox. Direct-vent piping runs up through the existing chimney chase. The original firebox is effectively retired in place. You get cool-glass safety, sealed combustion, push-button operation, and significantly more usable heat output.

The Cosmo Insert, Escape, Provident, Supreme, and SupremeX are all gas inserts designed for this scenario.

## How to pick

Choose a log set if your existing opening is structurally sound and you primarily want the look of a real fire without the wood management. Choose an insert if you want maximum usable heat output, sealed combustion, or the existing firebox is showing wear.

Your authorized dealer can assess the existing opening and recommend the right path during a free in-home consultation.
