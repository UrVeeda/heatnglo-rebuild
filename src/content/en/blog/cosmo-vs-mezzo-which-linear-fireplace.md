## Direct answer

The Mezzo and the Cosmo are both contemporary linear direct-vent gas fireplaces with the same core engineering. The difference is glass proportion:

1. **The Mezzo is wider than it is tall** -- optimized for horizontal media-wall installations, standard to elevated ceiling heights
2. **The Cosmo has a taller glass aperture** -- built for rooms with elevated ceilings, high wall paneling, and architectural vertical space
3. **Room dimensions decide the unit** -- not brand preference, dealer suggestion, or hero photography

Pick the one whose proportion matches the wall. Confirm the size with your authorized dealer before the rough-in is set.

## When to specify the Mezzo

The Mezzo is the workhorse of Heat & Glo's linear lineup. Its horizontal glass proportion reads as a thin ribbon of fire -- the aesthetic that defined the linear fireplace category when it became the default for residential great rooms.

### Wide horizontal walls

The Mezzo 48, 60, and 72 are the standard specs for walls in the 10 to 14-foot range. The horizontal proportion reads natural against a wide wall. It doesn't fight the architecture -- it echoes it.

### Standard and elevated ceiling heights

An 8 to 10-foot ceiling doesn't have the vertical real estate to justify the Cosmo's taller aperture. The Mezzo's lower profile sits correctly within the room's proportions.

### Media wall configurations

The Mezzo 48 or 60 below a mounted TV is one of the most-specified configurations in contemporary residential. The horizontal ribbon of fire sits as a base element under the screen without visual conflict. Both elements are horizontal. The room stays organized.

### Transitional and contemporary interiors

Whether the surrounding design is white oak slat walls, large-format porcelain, or honed limestone slab, the Mezzo's geometry integrates cleanly. Its width gives you flexibility in how you frame the wall around it.

## When to specify the Cosmo

The Cosmo is specified when the room has vertical space that needs to be honored, not ignored. Its taller glass proportion gives the flame room to move upward -- which is what you want in a 12-foot or taller room.

### Elevated ceilings and tall wall paneling

When the wall runs from floor to 12, 14, or 16 feet of ceiling, the Mezzo's horizontal proportion can look undersized. The Cosmo's taller aperture scales to the room. The fire reads as an architectural element, not an afterthought.

### Hospitality and commercial applications

Hotel lobbies, resort common areas, and upscale restaurant features frequently specify the Cosmo because the room height demands a taller firebox expression. The Cosmo's proportion is built for those scale relationships.

### Formal living rooms and double-height entries

A double-height entry with the fireplace as the focal point works with the Cosmo. The vertical emphasis of the flame matches the vertical drama of the space.

### High wall paneling configurations

Floor-to-ceiling stone slab, full-height fluted tile, continuous wood paneling -- these vertical wall treatments pair with the Cosmo's taller aperture. The fire reads as part of the vertical composition rather than sitting below it.

## What to do when neither is the right answer

### Partition walls and see-through configurations

If the brief is a fireplace that reads from two rooms -- a great room on one side, a dining room or kitchen on the other -- neither the Mezzo nor the Cosmo in standard configuration is the right solve. The [Primo II Linear Gas Fireplace](/fireplaces/primo-ii/) is offered in see-through configurations across its size range. A see-through Primo II is almost always the better specification for a shared partition wall.

### Smaller rooms and secondary spaces

Powder rooms, primary bedroom features, and compact living rooms don't always need a 48-inch linear unit. Confirm the room dimensions with your dealer before selecting size. Proportion applies to room-to-firebox scale, not just wall-to-firebox scale.

## How to choose: the three questions

Before you or your designer decides, answer these:

1. **What is the wall width?** Walls under 10 feet usually spec a smaller unit. Walls over 12 feet have room for a 60 or 72.
2. **What is the ceiling height?** Under 10 feet, the Mezzo's proportion is correct. Above 12 feet, the Cosmo's taller aperture scales appropriately.
3. **What is the firebox's role in the room?** If it anchors a media wall, the Mezzo. If it IS the room's focal point without competing elements, the Cosmo.

Then call your authorized dealer, confirm the size, and lock the rough-in dimensions before framing starts.

## Frequently asked questions

### What sizes do the Mezzo and Cosmo come in?

Both the Mezzo and Cosmo ship in multiple sizes. Your authorized dealer can confirm the current size range and which sizes are available in your region. Confirm size availability before locking rough-in dimensions into the construction drawings.

### Can I install a Mezzo or Cosmo myself?

No. Both are direct-vent gas appliances that require professional installation by a licensed contractor. Gas line connection, venting, and electrical hook-up for the IntelliFire ignition system must be performed by qualified tradespeople. Your authorized Heat & Glo dealer can coordinate installation.

### Do the Mezzo and Cosmo both work with the IntelliFire app?

Yes. Both fireplaces are IntelliFire-compatible, which means app-based control from a smartphone, compatibility with voice assistants, and scheduling capability. Your dealer can confirm the specific control package for your installation.

### What's the venting configuration for both units?

Both are direct-vent systems using a co-axial pipe that draws combustion air from outside and exhausts through the same assembly. No chimney required. The specific vent run length and configuration limits vary by unit and installation -- your dealer will confirm this during the consultation.

### Which unit has more flame options?

Both fireplaces offer multiple media and flame presentation options -- logs, crystals, glass beads, ember beds. The specific options vary by size and configuration. Your dealer can walk through the full media library for your chosen unit.

### Can either fireplace be installed as a two-sided or peninsula unit?

Not in standard configuration. For see-through, three-sided, or peninsula installations, the [Primo II](/fireplaces/primo-ii/) is the correct product. Specify the Primo II when the design calls for fire visible from more than one angle.
