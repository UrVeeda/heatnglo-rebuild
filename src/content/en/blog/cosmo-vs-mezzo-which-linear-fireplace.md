Both are contemporary linear direct-vent gas fireplaces. Both ship in multiple sizes. Both share the same fundamental engineering platform. The decision between them is proportion, and proportion is everything when the fireplace is the room's organizing element.

## When the Mezzo wins

Wide horizontal walls. Low to standard ceiling heights. Rooms where the fire wants to read as a thin line rather than a vertical statement. Most great-room media-wall installs land on a Mezzo 48 or 60 because the proportion sits right under a TV without visual conflict.

## When the Cosmo wins

Elevated ceilings. High wall paneling. Rooms where the design intent calls for the fire to honor the vertical space rather than just occupy it. Hospitality lobbies, formal living rooms, double-height entry spaces. The Cosmo's taller glass proportion gives the fire room to breathe upward.

## When you should bring in a third option

If you're staring at a partition wall between two rooms and asking which side gets the fireplace, neither single-sided product is the answer. The Primo II is offered in see-through configurations across its size range, and a see-through Mezzo or Primo II is almost always the better solve for that brief.

## What you do not decide based on

Brand reputation, hero photography, or your dealer's preference. Decide based on the wall and the ceiling. Then call your authorized dealer to confirm the right size for your room dimensions.
