# Heat & Glo — Marketing Claims Audit
**Audit date:** 2026-05-01
**Auditor:** Inspectah Deck (Verification Chamber, Claude Code era)
**Ledger audited:** `docs/data/marketing-claims.md` (8 claims)
**Commit context:** Two claims removed in `e71e631` ("12,400+ verified owners" and "3 dealers within 25 mi" proximity text) prior to this audit. Those are excluded here as already resolved. The 8 claims below are the remaining unverified ledger entries.

---

## Audit Summary

Eight claims from the 243-URL crawl were audited. Each source URL was fetched live and cross-checked against independent public sources where available. No claim cleared the ✅ Verified bar without at least one primary or strongly credible corroborating source. Result: 2 claims are fully verified, 1 is verifiable-but-stale, 3 require HHT internal data to close, and 2 are flagged for immediate removal. The two removal-flagged claims present the highest launch risk: one contains a quantified performance number (25% radiant heat improvement) with no publicly available independent test data to support it, and one attributes a "safe and sound" safety endorsement to a competitor brand's marketing director -- not a regulatory body, not Heat & Glo engineering -- while the technology it endorses is actively banned in Massachusetts, California municipalities, and several other jurisdictions. Both are still live on heatnglo.com and are still referenced in our content build context.

## Methodology Notes

**Sources trusted and why.** The primary trust tier was HHT's own authenticated site pages (`/history-and-heritage`, `/about-us/sustainability`), which carry corporate accountability weight. The founding year (1975) and milestone timeline came directly from HHT's history page and are treated as high-confidence. The FireBrick patent year (1995) came from the same timeline and was corroborated by the product page language ("introduced nearly 20 years ago," written circa 2015-ish, which resolves to a mid-1990s introduction). For the Trending Today claim, the subject's own website was treated as self-reported evidence only; the absence of any independently verifiable awards, air schedules, or production company disclosure drove the HHT-referral recommendation rather than a firm removal call -- though removal is the safer default if HHT can't produce documentation. For the vent-free claim, the legal geography of vent-free bans (Massachusetts statewide, California patchwork, Canada restrictions) is well-documented in HVAC regulatory literature and does not require a single authoritative citation to stand as a finding -- the page's own text acknowledges the bans exist, which is sufficient to flag the "safe and sound" framing as misleading.

**What this audit could not access.** The Hewing Hotel's official site returned connection errors across multiple URL variants. The Minnesota State Preservation Society project page was not accessible. Brandi Hagen's studio site redirected to a 404. These are minor gaps because none of the blocked pages carry claims central to the rebuild's homepage or product content. Google Patents search for the specific FireBrick ceramic formulation did not turn up a current active patent -- but absence from a keyword search is not a definitive finding; a design-around patent or continuation patent is possible. The 2.4M hearths installed figure and 1,400+ authorized dealers figure in the current `index.json` TrustStrip were NOT in the original 8-claim ledger and therefore out of scope for this audit, but both should be separately verified against HHT data before launch. Confidence on those two figures is currently unrated.

**Where confidence is highest vs lowest.** Highest confidence: the founding year (1975) and "50 years" framing -- primary source, company-confirmed. The vent-free removal recommendation -- the page text itself concedes the bans exist, which makes the "safe and sound" lead-in indefensible regardless of what Usilton's employer sells. Lowest confidence: the Jackson Building age discrepancy and the Trending Today award legitimacy -- both require HHT internal records that weren't publicly accessible.
