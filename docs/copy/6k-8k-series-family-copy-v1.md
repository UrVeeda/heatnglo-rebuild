## FAQ

**Q: What does "traditional" mean in the 6K/8K context, and how is it different from the Modern variant?**
A: The 6K/8K Series Traditional features a brick-lined firebox interior and a traditional log set arrangement, which together produce the warm, familiar hearth aesthetic that most American homeowners picture when they think "gas fireplace." The 6K/8K Series Modern, which is a separate product page, offers a different firebox interior treatment and a contemporary media presentation suited to cleaner-lined rooms. If your project calls for a recognizable hearth with classic proportions, the Traditional is the right specification. If the interior design is more contemporary, compare the Modern variant before finalizing.

**Q: Is the 6K/8K a good choice for new construction?**
A: Yes. The 6K/8K Series is one of Heat & Glo's highest-volume specifications for production and custom residential new construction. Its range of sizes covers the most common framing openings, installation documentation is well-established in the building trades, and authorized dealers maintain reliable inventory. For builder and contractor accounts, the Heat & Glo ProAdvantage trade program offers wholesale access, BIM files, and dedicated spec support.

**Q: What does my framing contractor need before rough-in?**
A: The 6K/8K is a factory-built zero-clearance direct-vent gas fireplace, frameable into a combustible wall assembly within the clearances specified in the installation manual. Before rough-in, your team needs the unit's width, height, and depth dimensions plus the listed clearance requirements. These are available through your authorized dealer or the ProAdvantage trade program. Note: dimensional values for 6K and 8K sizes are pending final HHT spec sheet publication in this listing and should be confirmed with your dealer before framing begins.

**Q: What venting options does the 6K/8K Series support?**
A: The 6K/8K Series is a direct-vent gas fireplace, drawing combustion air from outside and exhausting through a co-axial pipe system with no traditional chimney required. The sealed firebox maintains indoor air quality and keeps the glass clean. Your dealer or the project's mechanical engineer can confirm vent routing options and termination requirements for your specific installation. For projects with constrained vent paths, ask your dealer about power-vent availability.

**Q: How do I surround the 6K/8K with a mantel?**
A: The 6K/8K's traditional firebox proportions pair naturally with corbeled wood mantels, stone surrounds, and brick face extensions. The critical step is confirming clearance-to-combustibles requirements from the installation manual before any mantel material is specified or installed. Heat & Glo's mantel shelf and wood mantel collections include profiles designed for compatibility with this series. For custom masonry or tile surrounds, your installer should verify the installation manual's clearance requirements against the proposed material and distance from the firebox opening.

**Q: Can I add a remote control or smart-home integration to the 6K/8K?**
A: The 6K/8K Series is compatible with Heat & Glo's IPI (Intermittent Pilot Ignition) system, which supports remote control operation. Specific remote and smart-home compatibility options depend on the exact model and your installation configuration. Your authorized dealer can confirm which control accessories are available for the size you're specifying and advise on integration with popular home automation systems.
