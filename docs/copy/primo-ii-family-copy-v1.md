### Single-Sided Configuration

**Primo II 36**
The 36 single-sided is the right specification for a focused living room accent wall, a bedroom fireplace, or any space where a contemporary linear fire needs to feel purposeful without consuming the wall.

**Primo II 48**
The 48 single-sided anchors a great-room media wall, a dining room feature wall, or an open-plan living space where the fireplace is the room's organizing element without requiring the drama of a full see-through installation.

**Primo II 60**
The 60 single-sided is a room-commanding specification for large open-plan interiors, high-ceiling great rooms, and primary living spaces where the fireplace needs horizontal presence to stay visually balanced against the scale of the room.

### See-Through (Double-Sided) Configuration

**Primo II 36 Double-Sided**
The 36 see-through works well as a partition fire between a primary bedroom and a bath, or between a compact study and an adjacent sitting room, where the fireplace reads as a shared object rather than a feature of one space.

**Primo II 48 Double-Sided**
The 48 see-through is the specification to reach for when a living room and dining room share a wall and the fire should belong to both. It creates a visual connection between spaces without compromising the sense of separation.

**Primo II 60 Double-Sided**
The 60 see-through is built for wide-open floor plans: great rooms connected to kitchens, hotel lobbies separated from bar seating, residential open plans where a single fire needs to anchor two distinct zones simultaneously.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the difference between the single-sided and see-through Primo II?**
A: The single-sided Primo II has a firebox that opens to one room, with a back panel behind the flame. The see-through (double-sided) model has glass on both faces of the firebox, so the fire is visible from two adjacent spaces simultaneously. These are distinct product configurations with different framing envelopes, clearance requirements, and vent routing. A see-through installation requires a true partition wall or framed chase that separates the two spaces, and the installation documentation differs from the single-sided model. Confirm your configuration early, before framing begins, because converting between them post-installation is not a field modification.

**Q: Can the see-through Primo II work in a partition wall between a bedroom and a bathroom?**
A: Yes, the see-through configuration is a frequent specification for that exact pairing, as well as bedroom-to-sitting room dividers, dining room-to-living room separations, and any interior partition where a shared fire adds design value. Before finalizing, confirm that your specific wall assembly, clearance requirements, and local building codes permit a gas fireplace in that location. Your authorized dealer and the project's mechanical or HVAC contractor can advise on code requirements and vent routing for the double-sided configuration.

**Q: What does my framing contractor need before the Primo II goes in?**
A: Both configurations are factory-built zero-clearance direct-vent gas fireplaces, frameable into a combustible wall assembly within the clearances listed in the installation manual. For the see-through model, the chase or partition framing must accommodate the full depth of the firebox plus rear clearances. Your team needs the unit's width, height, and depth dimensions plus configuration-specific clearance requirements before rough-in. These are available through your authorized dealer or the ProAdvantage trade program. Note: dimensional values for Primo II sizes are pending final HHT spec sheet publication in this listing and should be confirmed with your dealer before framing.

**Q: What venting options does the Primo II support?**
A: The Primo II is a direct-vent gas fireplace, drawing combustion air from outside and exhausting through a co-axial pipe system with no chimney required. The sealed firebox maintains indoor air quality and glass clarity. Your dealer or the project's mechanical engineer can confirm vent routing options, run lengths, and termination requirements for your installation. For constrained vent paths, ask your dealer about power-vent availability. Note that vent routing requirements for the double-sided configuration may differ from the single-sided model.

**Q: How does Cosmo or Mezzo compare to the Primo II as an alternative?**
A: All three are contemporary linear direct-vent gas fireplaces from Heat & Glo, and all operate from the same fundamental design platform. The primary distinction is proportion and configuration availability. Mezzo is a thin-frame, low-profile linear suited for wide horizontal wall treatments. Cosmo shares the linear language but carries a taller glass proportion for high-ceiling contexts. Primo II is the family to reach for when see-through (double-sided) is either a requirement or a genuine option, since it is offered in both configurations from a single product line. Your dealer can help you compare installation envelopes if the room proportions are close between families.
