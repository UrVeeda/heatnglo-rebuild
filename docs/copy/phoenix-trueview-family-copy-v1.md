## FAQ

**Q: What does "open" mean for the Phoenix TrueView, and how is it different from a standard gas fireplace?**
A: Most gas fireplaces have a sealed glass front panel that separates the firebox from the room. That glass keeps combustion air and exhaust separated from indoor air, which is why direct-vent gas fireplaces maintain indoor air quality. The Phoenix TrueView is an open-hearth design: there is no glass panel between the fire and the room. The result is an experience that closely mirrors a traditional wood-burning open fireplace, including direct radiant heat output and an unobstructed view and presence of the flame. The venting system and gas combustion are engineered to operate safely without the sealed glass front. Confirm local code requirements with your dealer and the authority having jurisdiction, as open-hearth gas fireplaces are subject to specific installation and clearance rules.

**Q: Is the Phoenix TrueView as safe as a glass-front gas fireplace?**
A: The Phoenix TrueView is a listed gas appliance, engineered and tested to applicable safety standards. Like all open-hearth fireplaces, it produces radiant heat that extends into the room, which is part of its appeal and part of why its clearance-to-combustibles requirements are specific and non-negotiable. Your installer must follow the installation manual's clearance requirements exactly. A safety screen is a standard accessory for open-hearth fireplaces and is strongly recommended for households with children or pets. Your authorized dealer can advise on available screen options and required clearances for your specific installation.

**Q: What venting system does the Phoenix TrueView use?**
A: Because the Phoenix TrueView does not have a sealed glass front, its venting configuration differs from a conventional direct-vent gas fireplace. Venting specifics should be confirmed with your authorized dealer and the installing contractor before rough-in begins. Confirm code compliance requirements with the authority having jurisdiction for your project's location. Do not assume the venting approach of a glass-front gas fireplace applies to the Phoenix TrueView.

**Q: Who typically specifies the Phoenix TrueView?**
A: The Phoenix TrueView is a specifier's product. It lands most frequently in high-end custom residential projects, boutique hospitality interiors, and anywhere the client has explicitly requested the open-fire experience rather than the contained look of a traditional direct-vent gas fireplace. Interior designers and architects who work in luxury residential and hospitality are the primary specifiers. Homeowners who arrive at this page have usually already decided they want open-hearth and are looking for confirmation that the Phoenix TrueView delivers it.

**Q: How does the Phoenix TrueView compare to a wood-burning open fireplace for heat output and maintenance?**
A: For heat output, the Phoenix TrueView delivers consistent, controllable radiant warmth without the variable combustion and draft dependency of a wood fire. You get the same open-fire experience with a single-button start, thermostat compatibility, and no ash management. You eliminate the need for chimney cleaning, wood storage, and the ongoing attention a wood fire requires. What you retain is the visual and spatial presence of an open hearth: the sound of the fire in the room, the unobstructed view of the flame, and the radiant heat reaching the people in the room rather than being filtered through glass.
