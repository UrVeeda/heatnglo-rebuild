## FAQ

**Q: What makes Cosmo different from the Mezzo Series?**
A: Both are contemporary linear direct-vent gas fireplaces from Heat & Glo, and they share a clean, low-profile design language. The key difference is glass proportion: the Cosmo's firebox opening carries a taller vertical dimension relative to its width, making it particularly well-suited to rooms with elevated ceilings, high-wall paneling, or a design intent that calls for more visual height in the fireplace. If your project is proportionally wide and low-ceiling, the Mezzo is worth comparing. If your room has height to work with and you want the fire to honor it, Cosmo is typically the right specification.

**Q: Is the Cosmo suited for commercial or hospitality applications?**
A: Yes. The Cosmo's taller glass proportion and clean contemporary geometry have made it a frequently specified product in hotel lobbies, club lounges, restaurant dining rooms, and high-end multifamily common areas. For commercial specifications, your dealer or the Heat & Glo ProAdvantage trade program can provide the documentation needed for code review, including installation manuals and specification sheets. Confirm code requirements and clearance-to-combustibles needs with the installing contractor and the authority having jurisdiction before finalizing the specification.

**Q: What does my framing contractor need before the Cosmo goes in?**
A: Cosmo is a factory-built zero-clearance direct-vent gas fireplace, meaning it can be framed into a combustible wall assembly within the clearances listed in the installation manual. The framing crew needs the unit's width, height, and depth dimensions plus the listed clearances before rough-in begins. These are available through your authorized dealer or the ProAdvantage trade program. Note: dimensional values for Cosmo sizes are pending final HHT spec sheet publication in this product listing and should be confirmed through your dealer before rough-in.

**Q: What venting options does the Cosmo support?**
A: The Cosmo Series is a direct-vent gas fireplace, drawing combustion air from outside and exhausting through a co-axial pipe system with no chimney required. The sealed firebox keeps indoor air quality unaffected and the glass clean. Your dealer or the project's mechanical engineer can confirm vent routing options, run lengths, and termination requirements for the specific installation. For projects with constrained vent paths, ask your dealer about power-vent availability.

**Q: How does the Cosmo integrate with stone surrounds, tile, and built-in millwork?**
A: The Cosmo's thin steel frame is designed to disappear into whatever surround material you bring to it. Large-format vertical stone and tall tile fields reinforce the upward proportion of the firebox rather than competing with it. For built-in millwork and custom cabinetry, the taller glass opening creates a natural focal point that anchors flanking shelving or display niches on either side. Confirm clearance-to-combustibles requirements against your specific surround and mantel materials before installation. The Heat & Glo stone set and mantel shelf collections include profiles compatible with Cosmo's surround depth.

**Q: Is Cosmo available in a see-through or double-sided configuration?**
A: This is a product detail to confirm with your authorized dealer, as configuration availability can vary by size and market. If your project requires a double-sided or see-through linear fireplace, your dealer can identify the appropriate Heat & Glo product and verify whether Cosmo's sizing and vent requirements fit your installation envelope.
