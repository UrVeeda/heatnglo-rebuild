# Seasonality Calendar

Month-by-month content + messaging shifts for Heat & Glo. The site doesn't sell the same way in February as it does in October. This calendar codifies what shifts when.

---

## The four seasonal modes

| Mode | Months | Hero focus | Conversion goal |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Pre-winter ramp** | August through October | Gas inserts, "warm before the cold" | Schedule install before peak season |
| **Heating season peak** | November through February | Indoor fireplaces, holiday hosting, glass + flame photography | Drive consultations, dealer locator visits |
| **Spring transition** | March through April | Wood + electric, mantel refresh, "next-winter planning" | Capture leads for fall installs |
| **Outdoor season** | May through July | Outdoor fireplaces, patio fire features, outdoor dining | Drive outdoor product page traffic |

These are starting points based on US continental climate. Regional dealer pages eventually overlay (e.g., Florida's outdoor season is year-round, Minnesota's heating season starts earlier).

---

## What shifts and where

| Site element | What changes by season | Where it's edited |
|---|---|---|
| Homepage hero | Headline + photography + CTA | CloudCannon → Pages → Home → Hero |
| Homepage TrustStrip | Stats vary (e.g., installs scheduled vs designs delivered) | CloudCannon → Pages → Home → Trust Strip |
| Browse-the-Lineup card order | Reorder gas / wood / electric / outdoor based on season | CloudCannon → Pages → Home → Browse Lineup |
| Promotion in Settings | Active promo text + rebate amount | CloudCannon → Settings → site → rebate |
| Featured blog post | Pin a season-relevant article on home + blog index | CloudCannon → Blog (`featured: true`) |
| Email capture / lead form text | "Schedule your spring fireplace plan" vs "Get a winter consultation" | CloudCannon → Pages → home → ConsultationBand |

---

## Annual planning rhythm

- **Q1 review** (January): assess last year's performance by season, plan content + promo calendar for the year
- **Quarterly cutover meeting** (4 per year, around the 15th of the month before each new mode): walk through what's changing on the homepage + which blog posts shift in/out
- **Monthly content refresh** (first Monday of each month): minor updates, seasonal photography swap, blog featured-post rotation

---

## What is NOT seasonal

Some content stays put year-round:
- Founder bio / "Engineered since 1975" claims
- Practice-area landing pages (gas / wood / electric / log-sets / accessories)
- FAQ structure (individual answers may evolve, framework doesn't)
- Dealer locator
- API integrations

Don't seasonalize what doesn't need it. Seasonality should mirror real buyer-intent shifts, not invented marketing rhythm.
